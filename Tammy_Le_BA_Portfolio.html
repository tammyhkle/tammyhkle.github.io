User Story Hierarchy: UC-13 Audit Evidence Automation
Theme ‚Üí Epic ‚Üí User Story format, Premera Blue Cross, Cybersecurity Compliance
Theme: Reduce manual audit burden and improve audit readiness across cybersecurity platforms
Epic: Automate RFI evidence collection for IT audit cycles to make evidence repeatable, complete, and audit-ready without manual intervention
US-001 Epic: Evidence Automation HIGH
As a cybersecurity engineer on the threat intelligence team, I want evidence collection for Tenable IO and Tenable SC to be automated so that I don't have to manually capture screenshots and compile documents for every audit cycle.
Acceptance Criteria
System automatically pulls required data from Tenable IO and Tenable SC on trigger
Output includes required criteria, timestamps, and proof of execution in auditor-specified format
Evidence is centralized in SharePoint and accessible to audit team
Process completes without manual intervention from engineering team
US-002 Epic: Evidence Automation HIGH
As an IT auditor conducting a compliance review, I want submitted evidence to consistently include proof of execution with timestamps and defined criteria so that I don't need to send follow-up RFIs requesting missing information.
Acceptance Criteria
All submitted evidence includes required timestamps matching audit criteria
Format is consistent across all submissions with no variation by team or engineer
Evidence is complete on first submission so no follow-up requests are required
Submission is traceable with routing and approval timestamps visible
US-003 Epic: Evidence Automation MEDIUM
As a compliance manager responsible for audit readiness, I want the evidence collection process to be reusable across audit cycles so that my team doesn't have to rebuild the process from scratch every time a new audit begins.
Acceptance Criteria
Automated workflow runs on each new audit cycle without configuration changes
Evidence repository organized by audit cycle for easy retrieval
User documentation exists so team can operate the process without the original builders
Solution is extensible so additional systems like Exabeam and Proofpoint can be added to the same framework

Insurance Billing Workflow: Influence Without Authority
Lynnwood Vision Center, Healthcare Operations, 2018‚Äì2025
SITUATION At Lynnwood Vision Center, I started noticing a pattern insurance claims would occasionally come back with small errors. Nothing dramatic, but enough to cause rework and delayed reimbursements.
TASK I wasn't the doctor or the billing manager, but I wanted to reduce those delays and improve predictability for the practice.
ACTION Instead of pointing fingers, I tracked denial patterns and brought concrete examples to the doctor. I identified that small inconsistencies in eligibility documentation upstream were causing downstream billing corrections.

I framed it simply: "If we standardize this step, we may reduce rework and stabilize reimbursement timelines."

I mapped the verification-to-claim workflow, created a standardized checklist, aligned documentation steps across all staff, and built onboarding materials so every person followed the same structure from day one.
RESULT Claim errors decreased significantly, rework dropped, and reimbursements became more predictable. The practice moved from reactive corrections to a consistent, repeatable billing process.
Takeaway: Influence isn't about authority it's about data, framing impact clearly, and aligning around shared goals. That's the same pattern I bring to every cross-functional engagement.
Account Governance Audit: Risk Prioritization and Sustainability
APACE Nonprofit, Business Analyst & Webmaster, 2022‚ÄìPresent
SITUATION When I joined APACE, a volunteer-run nonprofit, they had around 77 accounts across two domains and no governance process. Former members still had active access, permissions didn't align with current roles, and nobody had the bandwidth to address it.
TASK I needed to reduce security risk without overwhelming a small volunteer team that had no dedicated IT function and limited time.
ACTION I conducted a full account audit across all 77 accounts, categorized each issue by risk level, and prioritized remediation based on exposure severity. I focused first on former member accounts still active and permissions exceeding current role requirements.

I remediated 25 accounts roughly a third and documented a repeatable quarterly governance process so the risk wouldn't accumulate again. I also streamlined volunteer intake by standardizing onboarding steps and automating notification workflows.
RESULT Security risk was significantly reduced. The organization moved from reactive one-time cleanup to structured, repeatable governance maintainable by a volunteer team without ongoing technical support.
Takeaway: The right solution isn't always the most advanced one it's the one that's sustainable given real constraints. Evaluating options against cost, usability, and maintainability is a discipline I carry into every role.
